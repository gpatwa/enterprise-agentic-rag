The problem

Enterprise work is split across search, BI, dashboards, tickets, docs, and warehouses. Employees ask one question, then chase five systems to trust the answer.

Search finds documents, not answers.

Enterprise search returns ten links and a hint. The user still has to open each, parse it, and stitch the answer together.

BI answers known questions, not new ones.

Pre-built dashboards cover yesterday's question. Today's question goes to a Slack thread and a ticket queue.

AI chat answers without enough governance.

Generic chatbots invent column names, ignore your business rules, and leave no audit trail. The CFO can't act on that.

How it works

Four layers that turn questions into trusted action.

01 Connect every source

Hybrid retrieval across structured, unstructured, graph, code, and multimodal data.

Vector search, knowledge graphs, structured SQL, semantic-layer views, and image embeddings — all routed by a planner that knows when to use what. 

# Compass selects the right tool for the question
user: "Why did Q2 revenue miss in EMEA?"

↓ planner routes through:
  · vector retrieval (PRD, OKR docs)
  · structured SQL (warehouse)
  · graph (account ownership)
  · code lineage (revenue model)

↓ context layer applies:
  · finance "revenue" definition
  · EMEA region mapping
  · close-of-quarter rule
Context applied · before the answer
DEFINITION Revenue
SUM(payment_value) WHERE order_status = 'delivered'
RULE Recognition policy

Recognized at delivery, not purchase. Returns excluded.

LINEAGE orders model

Salesforce → Fivetran → dbt → Postgres · refreshed every 1h.

PERMISSION Row-level scope

EMEA-only rows visible to user role finance.regional.

02 Apply business context

Definitions, rules, lineage, permissions, and freshness — before the answer.

Compass injects your glossary, business rules, code lineage, and policy at query time, so "revenue" or "active customer" mean what they mean at your company — not what the model guessed.

03 Answer with proof

Every response ships with citations, SQL, confidence, and audit.

Show the CFO the SQL behind the number. Show the auditor the row that produced the citation. Show the security lead the user, tenant, role, and time of every query. No black boxes.

  • Inline citations with source documents
  • Generated SQL with execution plan + rows scanned
  • Confidence score + reasoning trace
  • Audit log: who, what, when, which sources
Citations 3 docs

Actions are typed, scoped, and rollback-safe. Compass never executes without a configured approval policy and full audit logging.
04 Execute with controls

Take governed action — with approvals, RBAC, and rollback.

Compass can write back. Generate a board-deck export. Open a ticket on the right team. Notify the data owner of an anomaly. Trigger a webhook. All scoped by role, gated by approval, and logged for audit.
